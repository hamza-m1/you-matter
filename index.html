About Us

Welcome to You Matter, your trusted resource for mental health awareness and support. Our mission is to provide accurate information, practical advice, and a supportive community for individuals seeking to understand and manage their mental well-being.

At You Matter, we believe that mental health is just as important as physical health. We are dedicated to breaking the stigma surrounding mental health challenges and promoting open conversations about emotional well-being.

Common Mental Health Challenges

Stress

  • What is it? Stress is the body’s natural response to pressure or challenging situations. Short-term stress can be motivating, but ongoing stress can harm mental and physical health.

  • Common Causes: Work demands, financial worries, relationship difficulties, major life changes, or health concerns.

  • Signs: Irritability, headaches, muscle tension, difficulty sleeping, fatigue, and feeling overwhelmed.

Depression

  • What is it? Depression is a mood disorder that causes persistent sadness and loss of interest, affecting how you think, feel, and act.

  • Common Causes: Trauma, stressful life events, genetics, chemical changes in the brain, or prolonged illness.

  • Signs: Low energy, hopelessness, changes in sleep or appetite, difficulty concentrating, withdrawal from activities, and feelings of worthlessness.

Anxiety

  • What is it? Anxiety is more than everyday worry — it’s persistent fear or unease that interferes with daily life.

  • Common Causes: Ongoing stress, trauma, genetics, health conditions, or major life transitions.

  • Signs: Restlessness, racing thoughts, rapid heartbeat, sweating, difficulty sleeping, and avoiding situations that trigger worry.

Burnout

  • What is it? Burnout is emotional, physical, and mental exhaustion caused by prolonged stress, often linked to work or caregiving.

  • Common Causes: Heavy workloads, lack of rest, feeling undervalued, poor work-life balance, or constant pressure.

  • Signs: Fatigue, irritability, reduced motivation, difficulty focusing, detachment from responsibilities, and feeling drained.

Tools and tips

Regular sleep, meals, and daily activities provide structure and stability. A consistent routine helps regulate your body’s natural rhythms, reduces uncertainty, and makes it easier to manage stress.

Even small habits, like waking up at the same time or planning a daily walk, can create a sense of control and calm.

Gentle exercise like walking, stretching, or yoga boosts mood and energy. Physical activity releases endorphins, which improve emotional well-being, and helps reduce tension in the body.

You don’t need intense workouts — even short bursts of movement throughout the day can make a difference.

Talking to friends, family, or support groups helps reduce isolation and builds resilience. Sharing your thoughts and feelings can lighten emotional burdens, while listening to others fosters connection.

Even a quick chat or message can remind you that you’re not alone.

Being outdoors, even briefly, can lower stress and improve well-being. Natural environments encourage relaxation, reduce anxiety, and restore focus.

Whether it’s a park, garden, or simply opening a window to feel fresh air, connecting with nature can refresh your mind.

Reducing caffeine, alcohol, or excessive screen time can ease anxiety and improve sleep. These habits often disrupt rest and increase stress levels.

Setting boundaries, like screen-free evenings or limiting stimulants, helps your body and mind recharge more effectively.

Journaling, drawing, or talking about your feelings helps release tension and clarify emotions. Creative expression provides an outlet for thoughts that may feel overwhelming.

Writing down worries or sketching how you feel can make emotions easier to process.

Short pauses during the day prevent burnout and help you reset mentally. Breaks allow your mind to rest, improve focus, and reduce fatigue.

Even five minutes of stretching, breathing, or stepping away from a task can restore energy and perspective.

If challenges feel overwhelming, speaking to a professional can provide guidance and support. Therapists, doctors, or helplines can offer tools and reassurance tailored to your needs.

Asking for help is a sign of strength, and support is always available.

Resources

The NHS provides professional mental health care, including urgent help, talking therapies, and guidance on how to access local services.

Mind is a leading UK charity offering clear information, helplines, and local support for anyone experiencing mental health problems.

Samaritans are available 24/7 to listen and provide confidential support if you’re feeling overwhelmed or in crisis. You can call them free on 116 123.

Rethink offers advice, information, and support for people severely affected by mental illness, as well as resources for carers and families.
